LLM NPC social reasoning harness

Evaluating Social Reasoning in LLM NPCs through a Controlled Werewolf Game Harness

An exploratory study of interdisciplinary scaffolding under output-quality control.

Abstract

This project presents a seed-controlled 7-player Werewolf harness for evaluating observable social-reasoning behavior in LLM NPCs. The harness separates raw game-performance metrics from display-level output hygiene, then compares a baseline condition with a village-side interdisciplinary scaffold.

In STEP7-M RT2.3 N=20, the scaffold arm preserved raw quality pass 20/20 and replacements 0 while showing positive but small deltas in village win rate, role inference, vote consistency, and duration.

Final RT2.3 N=20 Result

Arm N Village win Role inference Vote consistency Quality pass
Baseline 20 0.200 0.317 0.334 20/20
Village scaffold 20 0.300 0.383 0.353 20/20
Delta - +0.100 +0.066 +0.019 +0

The result is exploratory. The role-inference delta does not reach the pre-defined +0.10 strong-claim gate.

Claim Boundary

Allowed: under output-quality control, the village scaffold showed exploratory positive trends in observable Werewolf behavior. Not allowed: statistical significance, general superiority, or human-level social reasoning.
